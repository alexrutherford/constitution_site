What is Constitutionology?

Constitutionology is a neologism for the application of exploratory data analysis to national constitutions. Techniques such as information retrieval and natural language processing allow the extraction of structure and insight from large collections of text data.

Why is it important?

Constitutions are an alternate lens through which to view history. The most important events of the last 200 years are reflected in the record of constitutional amendments. These include WWII and the collpase of the Soviet Union. The constitutions of former Empires still resemble one another.

We can also understand historical trends in constitutional amendments that are related to specific laws.

Where is the data from?

Three different datasources were used for the analysis presented on this site

  • English translations of the current constitutions from the Constitute Project

  • Expert tagging of 390 different constitutional provisions in the same documents

  • Historical constitutional amendment data from the Comparative Constitutions Project
